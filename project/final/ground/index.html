GROUND

/ground/ | verb

dictionary definition: to give (something abstract) a firm theoretical or practical basis

This activity will prompt you with questions revolving around the word “ground”. Consider conceptual, rather than literal meanings

examples can include:(your) mind, soul, spirituality, self-worth, in tenderness, in softness, in compassion, in community

*Note: your answers do not have to revolve around any of these, they are simply here to guide you into thinking deeper about what “ground” can mean to you.

Creator's Interpretation

To ground something is to give it roots so that it can flourish, so that it can grow to reach its full potential. Grounding requires compassion, patience, and a soft push away from shame or other negative cycles.
